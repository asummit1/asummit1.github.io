Projects

2020

FEMINISTUDENT Website and Resources for Women in Academia

I am currently developing a site that will serve to raise awareness of the plight of women in academia. This will include enlightening statistics that reflect the discrimination faced by women in various fields, as well as a list of books and podcasts that are organized by discipline and provide further insight and reflection on the topics of women and feminism. Finally, I plan to include an interactive map of the U.S. in which the user may click on the state where they attend university, find the university's name, and be taken direclty to their university's webpage on their Title IX and sexual harassment policies.



2019

The AI Safety Challenge at the Johns Hopkins Applied Physics Laboratory

This project was undertaken as part of the 2019 CIRCUIT cohort. In short, my team and I developed a set of four simulation environments, each built with the Unity game engine and addressing a different safety problem in contemporary AI research. These environments were packaged together and made available to the rest of the Intelligent Systems Center Laboratory at APL. Other researchers are able to test their algorithms directly in the environment to ensure that they are robust to safety risks.
